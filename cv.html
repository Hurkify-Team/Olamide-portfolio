About Me

Faith. Growth. Impact.

I was born in Lagos, Nigeria, in 1992, and raised in Mushin, a place that teaches strength early. Growing up as the third child in a family of twelve, in an environment that demanded resilience, I learned how to stand firm, adapt, and keep moving forward even when life was hard.

From a young age, I found purpose in computing and music. I explored technology with curiosity and expressed myself through Afrobeat, creating melodies simply because I loved it. Music became a lifestyle, while computing became a path. During my secondary school years, my passion for technology earned me a scholarship opportunity with NIIT, though financial limitations stopped me from taking it. That moment did not break me; it strengthened my resolve.

My journey beyond home led me to The Polytechnic of Iresi, Osun State, where I truly understood responsibility and adulthood. I later continued my education at the University of the People, completing a Bachelor's degree in Computer Science and further pursuing a double honour in Health Science. These experiences shaped my mindset and broadened my vision.

Today, my story is rooted in faith, growth, and impact. I believe that where I come from gives meaning to where I am going. My goal is to build, serve, and leave a legacy that proves difficult beginnings do not limit great outcomes.

"A sensible man may discover a way out, but it takes a brave man to walk that path and succeed."
